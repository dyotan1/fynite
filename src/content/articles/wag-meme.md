During every major football tournament, one three-letter word starts appearing across TikTok, Instagram, X, and sports headlines: **WAG**.

The term has been around for nearly two decades, yet every World Cup and European Championship introduces it to a new generation of fans.

## What does WAG mean?

**WAG** stands for **Wives and Girlfriends**.

It refers to the romantic partners of professional footballers.

Although the term is occasionally used in other sports, it is most closely associated with football.

## Where did WAG come from?

The term became widely known during the **2006 FIFA World Cup**.

British tabloids devoted huge amounts of coverage to England players' partners, particularly figures such as Victoria Beckham and Coleen Rooney. Their fashion, travel, and appearances often received as much attention as the football itself.

As media coverage grew, "WAG" became shorthand for footballers' partners.

The name has remained part of football culture ever since.

## Why is WAG trending again?

Major football tournaments always bring renewed interest.

Millions of fans now follow not only the matches but also behind-the-scenes content shared by players and their partners.

Popular videos include:

- Matchday outfits
- Stadium reactions
- Travel vlogs
- Family moments
- Celebrations after victories
- Fashion and beauty content

Social media has made these moments travel much faster than traditional television coverage ever could.

## What is WAG culture?

"WAG culture" describes the online fascination with the lives of footballers' partners.

Many have built successful careers as:

- Models
- Entrepreneurs
- Television presenters
- Influencers
- Business owners
- Athletes

Their content often attracts millions of views during international tournaments, making them internet personalities in their own right.

## Why is the term controversial?

Not everyone likes being called a WAG.

Critics argue that the label defines women by their relationship with a footballer instead of recognising their own achievements.

Many people covered under the label have spoken about wanting to be known for their own careers rather than simply as someone's partner.

Despite that criticism, the term continues to be widely used by fans and the media.

## Is WAG only used in football?

Mostly.

The word occasionally appears in sports such as rugby and cricket, but football is where it remains most recognisable.

For most people, hearing "WAG" immediately brings to mind major football tournaments.

## Why WAG remains popular

WAG has survived because it sits at the crossroads of football, celebrity culture, fashion, and social media.

Every major tournament creates new viral moments, introduces new faces to fans, and brings the term back into everyday conversation.

While some see it as an outdated label, others simply use it as convenient shorthand.

Either way, WAG has become one of football's most recognisable internet terms and is likely to keep resurfacing whenever the world's biggest tournaments return.
