Supporting a football team is usually harmless.

You wear the shirt.

You post about the match.

You hope they win.

Independent VTuber Essie tried the same thing during the 2026 World Cup.

The teams kept losing.

After enough eliminations, football fans reached a completely reasonable conclusion:

Essie was cursed.

## What is the Essie World Cup Curse?

The Essie World Cup Curse is an internet joke claiming that any national team publicly supported by VTuber Essie is destined to lose.

It spread during the knockout stage of the 2026 FIFA World Cup after fans noticed an increasingly unfortunate pattern.

Essie would support a team.

The team would lose.

She would move on to another country.

That team would lose too.

What started as coincidence quickly became a running internet conspiracy theory.

## Who is Essie?

Essie is an independent VTuber and online creator.

During the World Cup, her posts about the tournament began attracting attention from football fans outside her usual audience.

The reason was not tactical analysis or match predictions.

It was her apparent ability to support the wrong team at exactly the wrong time.

As the losses accumulated, screenshots of her posts became part of the joke.

Fans stopped asking who Essie wanted to win.

They started asking who was about to be eliminated.

## How did the curse start?

The meme developed during the World Cup knockout rounds in July 2026.

Essie publicly showed support for national teams including Brazil.

When those teams lost, people began connecting her posts to the results.

One loss is normal.

Two losses are unfortunate.

Keep repeating the pattern during the world's biggest football tournament and the internet starts writing supernatural lore.

By the Round of 16, the joke had become difficult to ignore.

Fans tracked Essie's support from match to match and treated every new team she backed as the curse's next target.

## Why were football fans begging Essie to stop?

Because the joke became interactive.

Once people knew about the supposed curse, every Essie post created immediate panic in the replies.

If she supported your country, the meme suggested, the damage had already been done.

Fans joked about protecting their teams from her support.

Others tried to direct the curse towards opponents.

The football itself was real.

The supernatural power obviously was not.

But pretending Essie could personally alter the World Cup bracket gave fans another story to follow between matches.

## Did Essie know about the curse?

Yes.

As the meme grew, Essie began playing along with the joke.

That changed the curse from something people were saying about her into a shared performance between Essie and football fans.

Every declaration of support became an event.

The replies arrived.

The warnings started.

Then everyone waited for the match.

If the team lost, the curse became stronger.

The meme was effectively writing its next episode live.

## Why did the Essie curse become so popular?

The timing was almost perfect.

World Cup knockout matches already have simple consequences.

Win and continue.

Lose and go home.

That made the supposed curse incredibly easy to track.

People did not need to understand VTubers.

They did not need to know Essie's content.

They only needed to understand one rule:

Essie supports team.

Team loses.

The more often the pattern appeared to repeat, the funnier the next prediction became.

## Did France break the Essie World Cup Curse?

Yes.

Eventually, the curse failed.

Essie supported France ahead of its World Cup quarter-final against Morocco.

France won 2-0.

Kylian Mbappé and Ousmane Dembélé scored as France advanced to the semi-finals.

After a run of teams associated with Essie's support being eliminated, France had survived.

The internet declared the curse broken.

For France, it was a World Cup quarter-final victory.

For Essie, it was finally evidence that publicly supporting a football team did not automatically destroy its tournament.

Probably.

## Was the curse ever real?

No.

The Essie World Cup Curse was a joke built around coincidence, selective attention, and an unusually entertaining sequence of football results.

There is no evidence that a VTuber posting support for a national team can influence a World Cup match.

That sentence should not need to be written.

The internet made it necessary.

But factual accuracy was never really the point of the meme.

The fun came from pretending the pattern was real and watching the next match to see whether it would continue.

## Why the Essie World Cup Curse worked

The best internet jokes often create a rule.

The [Ohio meme](/memes/what-does-ohio-mean) decided bizarre things happen in Ohio.

Huss Valley decided Daniel LaBelle keeps running through the same dangerous fictional place.

The Essie curse decided one VTuber should never be allowed to support your national team.

Once people understood the rule, every new post added to the story.

Essie picked a team.

Football fans panicked.

The match happened.

For a while, the curse kept winning.

Then France ruined everything by simply winning a football match.
