Open almost any TikTok comment section today.

Sooner or later, you'll find someone writing:

**"ts pmo."**

No punctuation.

No explanation.

Just three tiny letters that somehow seem to make perfect sense to everyone except you.

If you've been wondering what **TS PMO** actually means, you're not alone.

The phrase has become one of TikTok's favourite pieces of internet shorthand, and like most viral slang, it looks far more confusing than it actually is.

## What does TS PMO mean?

Most commonly, **TS PMO** means:

**"This shit pisses me off."**

Broken down, it looks like this:

- **TS** = This shit
- **PMO** = Pisses me off

Put together, it's simply another way of saying something is annoying, frustrating, or unbelievably irritating.

Someone might post a terrible opinion.

A football referee makes another questionable decision.

Your favourite character dies for no reason.

The comments fill with:

> **ts pmo 😭**

## Wait... doesn't PMO mean something else?

Yes.

This is where many people get confused.

Unlike most internet abbreviations, **PMO has two completely different meanings.**

The older meaning is:

**Put Me On.**

In that context, someone might say:

> "PMO good songs."

Meaning:

> Recommend me some good songs.

Or:

> "PMO your barber."

Meaning:

> Tell me where you get your haircut.

The meaning depends entirely on context.

However, once someone writes **TS PMO**, it almost always means:

**This shit pisses me off.**

## Why is everyone suddenly saying TS PMO?

The phrase itself isn't new.

The abbreviations have existed online for years.

What changed was TikTok.

As internet slang became faster and shorter, users started stacking abbreviations together.

Instead of writing:

> "This shit pisses me off."

People wrote:

> **ts pmo**

Soon that wasn't enough either.

Comments started looking like this:

> **icl ts pmo ngl**

Or:

> **ts pmo fr**

To anyone outside those communities, it looked like complete nonsense.

That confusion became part of the joke.

## What does TS actually mean?

Originally:

**TS = This shit.**

Over time, something funny happened.

Many users assumed it simply meant:

**This.**

So they began writing things like:

> "delete ts"

> "ts video"

> "ts song"

Technically, those phrases expand to:

> "delete this shit"

> "this shit video"

Which doesn't really make grammatical sense.

Eventually people noticed.

Then they kept doing it anyway.

Like many internet slang terms, the mistake became part of the language.

## Where did TS PMO come from?

Neither abbreviation was invented by TikTok.

Both had already been used online for years, particularly in communities influenced by **African American Vernacular English (AAVE).**

As those expressions spread across social media, millions of users adopted them without necessarily knowing where they came from.

TikTok accelerated the process.

Within months, abbreviations like **TS**, **PMO**, **ICL**, **ONB**, and **NGL** became everyday internet vocabulary.

## Why does internet slang keep getting shorter?

The internet loves efficiency.

LOL became ordinary.

People started using LMAO.

Then ROFL.

Now entire sentences are compressed into abbreviations.

Instead of saying:

> "This is really annoying."

Someone types:

> "ts pmo"

It saves time.

It also signals that you're familiar with the latest internet slang.

## How do people use TS PMO?

Most of the time, it's used as a reaction.

Someone posts an outrageous hot take.

Someone spoils the ending of a TV show.

Your favourite team somehow loses again.

The replies might simply read:

> "ts pmo"

Or:

> "bro ts pmo 😭"

The phrase doesn't always express serious anger.

Often it's exaggerated frustration, the kind of dramatic annoyance that internet comments are famous for.

## Why is TS PMO bigger than one piece of slang?

The popularity of TS PMO shows how quickly internet language evolves.

Every generation shortens words.

The internet shortens entire sentences.

Sometimes the abbreviations become so compressed that outsiders can't understand them anymore.

Ironically, that's exactly what makes them spread even faster.

People see the phrase.

They search it.

Then they start using it themselves.

**Today it's TS PMO. Next year, the internet will probably invent something even shorter.**
