Scroll through TikTok or Instagram Reels for long enough and you'll eventually run into something like this: an AI-generated shark wearing sneakers, narrated in Italian, with a name like Tralalero Tralala. It sounds like nonsense because it is nonsense — and that's the entire point.

## What it is

"Italian brainrot" is the umbrella term for a wave of surreal, AI-generated meme characters that emerged in early 2025. The characters are typically hybrids: animals fused with everyday objects, foods, or weapons — a shark with running shoes, an elephant with a cactus for a body, a cappuccino cup with a ballerina's legs. They're given Italian-sounding names, often built from nonsense words with Italian suffixes like "-ini" or "-ello," and are narrated with exaggerated, melodramatic AI-generated voiceovers in Italian.

Some of the better-known characters that came out of the trend include Tralalero Tralala (the sneaker-wearing shark), Bombardiro Crocodilo, Chimpanzini Bananini, and Ballerina Cappuccina.

## Where it came from

The trend's roots trace back to October 2023, when internet users made memes of actor and wrestler Dwayne Johnson delivering absurd rhyming lines, including the nonsense phrase "Tralalero tralala." That phrase later got reused as the basis for the character of the same name.

The character most widely credited as the actual starting point of Italian brainrot is Tralalero Tralala himself, who is generally traced to a TikTok account, @eZburger401, that posted a video featuring the character in January 2025 — a post that reportedly got the account banned, likely because of profanity in its audio. From there, other accounts picked it up: one user reposted the audio alongside an AI image of a shark-pigeon hybrid, and another posted a now-deleted video of a shark wearing shoes that reached seven million views in mid-January 2025. The exact origin point beyond that is genuinely disputed, since so many accounts were remixing the same audio and imagery within days of each other.

## Why it spread so fast

Italian brainrot is built to be endlessly extendable. Anyone with an AI image generator can invent a new hybrid animal, give it a ridiculous Italian-sounding name, and drop it into the same absurd universe — so the trend doesn't rely on one creator or one joke, it multiplies through an open-ended template. The characters are also visually distinct and easy to merchandise as stickers, plush toys, and fan art, which has helped keep them circulating well past a typical meme's shelf life.

The name itself points to a broader idea: "brain rot" was named Oxford's Word of the Year for 2024, describing the mental fog that comes from consuming an excess of low-effort, low-value online content. Many people who share Italian brainrot memes are doing so with a wink, using the term to acknowledge that they know exactly how ridiculous — and how much of a time sink — this content is, while still finding it funny enough to keep watching.

## Why it matters

Italian brainrot is one of the clearest recent examples of AI-generated content becoming genuinely viral on its own terms, rather than as a novelty. It shows how quickly a nonsense audio clip and a generative image tool can spawn an entire shared universe of characters, memes, and in-jokes — built collaboratively by strangers across different accounts, with no single person in charge of where it goes next.
