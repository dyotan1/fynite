Football fans expected goals.

The internet expected memes.

So far, the **2026 FIFA World Cup** has delivered both.

From Erling Haaland becoming the internet's "babygirl" to bizarre reaction images, AI edits, viral celebrations, and TikTok fan compilations, this World Cup has become just as entertaining off the pitch as it has been on it.

If your social media feeds suddenly feel like football meme accounts, you're definitely not the only one.

## Why are there so many World Cup memes?

Every World Cup produces memorable moments.

The difference in 2026 is how quickly those moments spread.

TikTok, X, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and AI image tools can turn a funny facial expression or celebration into a global meme within minutes.

Fans no longer wait until after matches.

The memes begin while the game is still being played.

## Erling Haaland became football's internet superstar

One of the biggest meme stories of the tournament has been **Erling Haaland**.

Despite being one of football's most intimidating strikers, fans began calling him **"babygirl."**

The nickname isn't meant literally.

In internet culture, "babygirl" is an affectionate and ironic label often given to male celebrities who appear unexpectedly awkward, funny, or emotionally expressive.

Haaland's filtered selfies, strange interviews, Shrek jokes, and relaxed personality made him perfect meme material.

## Celebrations become memes too

Goals aren't the only thing going viral.

Celebrations now spread almost as quickly.

Norway's famous **Viking Row** celebration inspired thousands of TikTok recreations.

Supporters, schools, and even sports teams outside football began copying it.

Soon the celebration became recognizable even among people who weren't watching the World Cup.

## AI has changed football memes

Unlike previous tournaments, many 2026 memes are AI-generated.

Fans create fake movie posters.

Animated reaction videos.

Alternate commentary.

Absurd crossover edits featuring footballers inside completely unrelated films and TV shows.

Most are obviously fictional.

That's part of the joke.

## Why does TikTok matter so much?

TikTok has become one of football's biggest discovery platforms.

Many younger fans don't watch an entire 90-minute match.

Instead, they discover highlights through short clips, funny edits, and reaction videos.

That means a player's personality can become almost as important as their goals.

## Football is becoming internet culture

The modern football fan doesn't separate sport from social media.

A player can score a hat-trick.

Minutes later they're trending because of a funny facial expression.

Friends become shipping edits.

Press conferences become reaction GIFs.

Missed chances become viral templates.

The football never stops.

Neither do the memes.

## Will these memes last?

Probably not forever.

Internet culture moves incredibly fast.

Most World Cup memes disappear after the tournament ends.

But a few become part of football history.

Think of them as snapshots of how fans experienced the competition online.

Years from now, people may forget individual matches.

They'll still remember the memes.

## The internet's World Cup

The 2026 FIFA World Cup isn't just being played in stadiums.

It's also being played across TikTok timelines, X threads, Instagram edits, and meme pages around the world.

Goals create headlines.

Memes create memories.

That's why this tournament feels less like a sporting event and more like one giant internet conversation.
