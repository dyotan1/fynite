Erling Haaland is 6 feet 5 inches tall.

He is one of the most intimidating strikers in football, spent his first World Cup scoring seven goals, and is regularly described as a machine.

Naturally, the internet looked at him and decided:

**Babygirl.**

If that sentence makes absolutely no sense, you have discovered one of the stranger ways online fandom talks about famous men.

## Why are people calling Haaland a babygirl?

The internet is not literally saying Erling Haaland is a baby or a girl.

In online fan culture, **"babygirl" can be an affectionate and deliberately ironic label for a male celebrity or fictional character.**

It is often applied when fans become attached to a man's softer, awkward, vulnerable, or unexpectedly goofy side.

The contrast makes the joke stronger.

Haaland is physically enormous.

On the pitch, he scores goals with machine-like efficiency.

Off the pitch and online, fans see filtered selfies, strange jokes, Shrek comparisons, funny interviews, and an athlete who appears unusually comfortable looking ridiculous.

The terrifying striker and the goofy internet personality are the same person.

That contradiction is exactly what the meme needs.

## Wait, what does "babygirl" mean in internet slang?

Traditional definitions will not help much here.

Online, calling a grown man "babygirl" is usually a form of affectionate fandom language.

The person does not need to be feminine.

In fact, the joke often works better when the label seems completely incompatible with the person receiving it.

A huge warrior.

A morally questionable fictional villain.

A tired middle-aged actor.

An elite Norwegian striker.

The internet can look at any of them and collectively decide they require emotional protection.

It is exaggerated affection with a layer of irony.

## Why Haaland specifically?

Haaland has always been unusually meme-friendly.

His facial expressions become reaction images.

His interviews produce strange quotes.

He has posted filtered selfies and joked about his appearance.

During the 2026 World Cup, he even leaned into online comparisons between himself and Shrek.

Fans also circulated clips of quieter moments: Haaland interacting with supporters, behaving awkwardly, laughing with teammates, and spending time with England star Jude Bellingham.

The internet did what the internet does.

It built a second version of Haaland.

Football sees a dominant striker.

Fan edits see a giant Norwegian man who must apparently be protected at all costs.

## The World Cup made the obsession explode

The babygirl jokes did not appear in isolation.

Haaland became one of the biggest internet personalities of the 2026 World Cup.

The Guardian reported that UK TikTok searches for "Haaland" increased by more than 300 percent week on week during the tournament.

Searches for "Haaland best moments" rose by 1,300 percent.

His Instagram following also surged during Norway's run.

And there was plenty of material to feed the algorithm.

Haaland scored seven goals as Norway reached the quarter-finals in their first World Cup appearance since 1998.

Norway's "Viking Row" celebration spread online.

Memes, AI-generated videos, fan edits, and clips of Haaland's personality moved far beyond normal football audiences.

People who did not follow Manchester City were suddenly being introduced to Erling Haaland through their feeds.

Many of them were not discussing tactics.

They were calling him babygirl.

## What does Jude Bellingham have to do with this?

Quite a lot, according to fan edits.

Haaland and Jude Bellingham were teammates at Borussia Dortmund, and their friendship has remained a popular subject for football fans online.

Clips and photographs of the pair are regularly edited into emotional friendship compilations.

Some fans go further and "ship" them, imagining a romantic relationship between the two.

That does not mean the fan-created stories are real.

It shows how modern sports fandom can borrow the language and behaviour of celebrity and fictional-character fandom.

Players are no longer discussed only through goals, assists, and trophies.

Their friendships, clothes, facial expressions, social posts, and tiny interactions can become entire internet narratives.

## Is Haaland in on the joke?

Haaland appears very aware that the internet finds him funny.

His online personality has often been self-deprecating and deliberately strange.

That matters.

A celebrity who aggressively tries to control every meme can make the joke feel uncomfortable.

Haaland frequently does the opposite.

He participates.

He posts the weird picture.

He acknowledges the Shrek comparison.

He allows the internet version of Erling Haaland to exist beside the footballer.

That gives fans even more material.

## Why is this bigger than one football meme?

The Haaland babygirl phenomenon shows how sports fandom is changing.

A generation raised on TikTok edits, stan accounts, reaction images, and fandom language does not suddenly abandon those habits when watching football.

The same vocabulary follows them into sport.

An athlete can be a Golden Boot contender and a meme character at the same time.

Fans can analyse a goal in one video and make a 20-second emotional edit of the scorer in the next.

For Haaland, the contrast is almost perfect.

He looks like football's final boss.

He scores like a machine.

And the internet has apparently decided he is its precious Norwegian babygirl.

**No, it is not supposed to make complete sense. That is probably why the nickname works.**
